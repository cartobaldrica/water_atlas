Water Use


Water use in Oregon is split into is regulated by the Water Resources Department in the form of water rights. Water rights are split into 13 categories, each of which outlines specific applications for water use. Irrigation is the largest category, comprising a majority of permits, but the extent of which varies by region.



Created by Gareth Baldrica-Franklin, Institute for Water and Watersheds, Oregon State University

















Total Water Use

Total water used by county per day based on 2010 USGS estimates. Counties with the highest levels of water use per day are typically areas where widespread irrigation is necessary. The daily average doesn't reflect how water use changes season-to-season, which is especially true in heavily irrigated areas.

Commercial/Industrial

The use of water related to the production or sale of goods or services. This could be from a private or public organization.
