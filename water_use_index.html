Water Use


Approximately a quarter of Oregonians live in floodplains. Despite the increased management and regulation of rivers, flooding remains a risk throughout most of the state, particularly west of the Cascades. Understanding the impacts of historic floods and surveying at-risk areas can aid hazard planning and response.

















Water Use

Mining

Use of water for the extraction, grading or processing of minerals or aggregates, as well as the use of water for construction and operations at mining sites.

Domestic

Use of water for human consumption, households or domestic animals that are sourced separately from municipal water utilities. Categories include applications for domestic landscaping, group use, school use, and basic human consumption.

Agriculture

Water for the production of agricultural products. This does not include irrigation for crops, rather, the use of water for agricultural operations, sanitation and maintenance at agricultural facilities. Some applications include dust control, flood harvesting, and animal waste management.

Irrigation

Use of water to grow or nourish crops or plants. This is primarily for agricultural purposes, but is also used for commercial gardens, golf courses and parks.

Commercial/Industrial

The use of water related to the production or sale of goods or services. This could be from a private or public organization. Water could be used specifically in the production of goods, or, more commonly, is used to support the maintenance and operation of commercial facilities (hotels, stores, hospitals, etc.).

Recreation

Use of water for fun and amusement. This could include boating, swimming, fishing or the support of natural systems and beauty. In many cases, water used by parks and recreation areas that isn't used specifically for watering plants is included in this category.

Power

Use of water to produce electricity or mechanical power. In Oregon, this is primarily for hydroelectric uses.

Fish

Water used for aquaculture or the protection of fish habitats.

Livestock

Water used for the maintenance of livestock supplies. This is primarily e water supplies directly used for livestock drinking supplies.

Municipal

Water used for servies provided by municipal organizations, typically in the form of water bureaus or authorities. Water from municipal providers can be used domestically, commercially, industrially and for irrigation. Municipal water cannot be used for the generation of hydroelectricity.

Instream

Water rights within the channels of streams and rivers. Instream rights are used to for temperature control, fisheries enhancement and fish propagation. They can also be used for recreational support.

Miscellaneous

Water used for applications not outlined in the above categories. Its two main sub-categories are fire protection and water storage. Other miscellaneous uses include road construction, pollution abatement and forest management.

Wildlife

Use of water for maintaining wild species and habitats. These rights are typically maintained in wildlife preserves and parks.
